## 📝 Reflection on Building My CV

### 📄 Creating My CV Structure  
When I started building my CV, I used another portfolio as a reference to guide my structure and content. This helped me understand how a professional CV should be organized, including sections like skills, projects, education, and experience.

At first, I found it challenging to balance technical and creative skills because my field includes both development and multimedia design. I had to carefully structure my CV so that it clearly presents both areas without making it look cluttered.

Through trial and adjustment, I improved the layout and ensured that each section was clear, consistent, and easy to read.

---

### 🛠️ Showcasing My Technical & Creative Skills  
One of the main challenges I faced was deciding how to present my diverse skill set. Unlike a purely development-focused CV, mine includes programming, databases, and design tools like Adobe Illustrator and Figma.

I had to think critically about grouping my skills into categories such as programming, frameworks, databases, and multimedia. This helped me present my abilities in a more professional and structured way.

This process improved my ability to organise information and present myself more effectively as a well-rounded ICT student.

---

### 📁 Presenting My Projects  
While adding my projects, I realised that simply listing them was not enough. I needed to describe what I actually did and what technologies I used.

I focused on explaining my role in each project, especially in the Student Enrolment System, where I applied Object-Oriented Programming and worked with data models. I also included my design work to show my creativity and practical experience.

This helped me understand the importance of clearly communicating my contributions and skills.

---

### 💼 Reflecting on Work Experience  
Including my work experience at Shoprite Warehouse was important because it shows that I have real-world experience, even outside the tech industry.

At first, I didn’t think it was very relevant, but I realised that it demonstrates important soft skills such as teamwork, efficiency, and attention to detail. These are valuable in any professional environment.

This helped me see how non-technical experience can still strengthen a CV.

---

### 🌐 Preparing for Portfolio Development  
Creating this CV helped me prepare for building a full portfolio. I now understand how to structure professional content and present my skills in a clear and organised way.

It also made me more confident in using tools like GitHub and structuring content for online presentation.

---

### 🔍 Overall Reflection  
This task helped me grow both technically and professionally. I improved my ability to organise information, present my skills clearly, and think critically about how I represent myself.

The biggest challenge was balancing my technical and creative abilities, but through careful structuring and refinement, I created a CV that reflects my strengths.

Overall, I now feel more prepared to present myself in the ICT industry and continue developing my skills.
